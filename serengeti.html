About Serengeti

The Serengeti National Park is one of Africa’s most famous wildlife conservation areas, renowned for its vast savannahs, diverse ecosystems, and the annual Great Migration. Located in northern Tanzania, the Serengeti spans over 14,750 square kilometers and is home to an incredible array of wildlife, including the Big Five—lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino. The park's name, derived from the Maasai word "Siringet," means "endless plains," a fitting description for its breathtaking landscapes.

Visitors to the Serengeti can witness one of nature's most spectacular events: the Great Migration, where over 1.5 million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles traverse the plains in search of fresh grazing grounds. Beyond the migration, the Serengeti offers year-round wildlife viewing, stunning sunsets, and a chance to experience the raw beauty of Africa's wilderness.
